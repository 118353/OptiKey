Styleguide

Basics

OptiKey’s site and design are built with Bootstrap v4. Many style options already exist within bootstrap, and can be customized if there is a specific need for OptiKey.

Bootstrap Documentation

Logo

The OptiKey Logo comes in three variants: the icon, the logo, and the word mark. Both light and dark versions are available

In all usages, the preferred color is black (#000000). Right click on these images to download vector (SVG) versions of the logos.

Icon

The Icon is a square 180px image. This is best used where icons are appropriate, such as a favicon, executable, or other areas where a square shape is needed

Logo

The logo contains the icon, plus the text “tiKey”, The logo is best used where the word mark would not be appropriate. An example is this website.

The font used in the logo is Monserrat Regular

Word Mark

The word mark is a bit bigger than the logo, and contains the text “Type Click Speak” giving more context. The word mark is best used in marketing material, emails, or in 2nd or 3rd party situations.

Font

The font used in the icons is Monserrat. The font used in the OptiKey app is Roboto. Roboto comes in many variants and weights, making it a good font to use for many circumstances.

Roboto Consdensed 500 is used in headings on this site. The display-* classes use weight 700. Roboto Regular 400 is used in body copy.

Main Colors

Variants

Other colors used in different contexts within the app are blue and orange. Blue is used to indicate text as it is typed, orange is used to indicate how long your eyes have been focused on an element. These colors are best used as compliments to main colors, sparingly, and never directly together.
